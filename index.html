Career

Before we jump into the meat of things here, I want to mention that I'll only be talking about my career as a Software Engineer here. If you want to see the rest of the job history, check out my Linked In.

Pharos Systems International - November 2017 to Present

I've worked on a wide variety of things at Pharos. Some of the highlights include:

  • Helping to improve our Cake build process
  • Redesigning and implementing our authentication and secure print client
  • Improving our Android app for Ricoh iMFPs
  • Building multiple PoCs
  • Microservices!
My team was responsible for the redesign and implementation of our authentication and secure print client. This was a fun and unique challenge because our client is browser-based in an effort to reduce the amount of client-specific logic we would have to write. This limitation in conjunction with the limitations of integrated Multi Functional Printer (iMFP) browsers resulted in us writing our own pure TypeScript web client app. The app has no runtime dependencies other than those required by certain vendors to interact with their iMFPs--which are only delivered to the client in the case that it's running on an iMFP that requires them. The resulting application is a lot more performant than the legacy application and is also a lot more streamlined. I'm very proud of the work my team and I put into this one.

Just Write

Just Write was a project that was meant to help encourage my client's writing-based Slack community to write more often. The application was a combination of Slack webhooks alongside a web app. Users could type simple commands in Slack to log the time the had spent writing, and then they would be provided with links to the web application to see a dashboard with charts, statistics, and insights about their writing habits. The first iteration and proof-of-concept was written in Ruby on the Ruby on Rails (RoR) framework. The final application was written in .NET Core. I made the decision to switch to .NET Core after the PoC since I'm very familiar with the .NET tech stack. Therefore, I felt more comfortable supporting and maintaining that than a RoR application.
